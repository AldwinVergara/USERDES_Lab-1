Visual Information Design

Aldwin D. Vergara | October 22, 2020

The first thing that we see and appreciate when we go see a painting, a structure, a project, or a website is its overall design. What we see visually is what attracts us to further interact or engage with the subject in front of us. And the art of graphic and visual design makes the information within visually appealing and be presented properly.


Last session’s topic is very interested because it talks about the aesthetic side of a production. I may not be that very good or talented with arts and design, but I do like learning and expanding my skills for that matter. With the topic discussed, I really learned a lot about the differences of colors, layouts, and typography which are the basic tools of a visual design. Before, I usually just put the types of colors, layouts, and topography in my work that “I” think that is visually appealing. Without planning and understanding the connotative meaning of a single color, shape, or font, I just insert what I feel that would best fit my art work or any of my work that concerns designing. I do have an idea that everything in a design has a particular meaning which my friends and family members who has a background in arts told me so and from the videos I sometimes watch, but I often forget what they say and just go with the flow of my own hands and mind.


Visual Information Design is important especially upon a creation of a website because this would make the users interact more with the site. The integration of information with the overall design makes it easier to present and express the information that the team wanted to convey. Every color is powerful and gives perception, every font/typeface build reputations, every grid system shows alignments, and every design present information. I am happy that I got to learn a somehow introduction to visual information design because I know that there’s a lot more about visual information design that I can expose myself into that would improve and develop my arty-side. And I’m very excited to try and apply it as a practice in my own personal portfolio or website for this course. I somehow managed to incorporate this topic for my website even without having to discuss it yet, but I would want to improve my work with what this topic suggests. I wanted to meet the three goals of visual design which are to guide the users along my site which conveys structure, relative importance, and relationships; to pace, attract the interest, and draw people in; and to express the information that I want to express that breathes life into the content. Just like what we did in activity 4 where we improved the content in a step-by-step process, I wish to improve my website to meet the goals. I really liked that activity because we had the chance to interpret the content and design it in our own ways and understanding. I really like designing a content because it’s visually appealing and I got to express what I feel and what I want to say.


Overall, visual information design plays a vital role upon a creation and development of a website and it’s an important thing that one should always consider. Of course, I may not be really good with it at first—which I accept and expect—but I do believe that practice makes everything perfect and permanent. I will try my best to incorporate visual information design in my website and I am looking forward to further expand my knowledge and apply it to more projects that I will face both in my remaining college years and when I venture the industry.
