Color Theory and Typography

Aldwin D. Vergara | November 15, 2020

This day was the time when we play four games concerning color theory and typography as an alternative for our synchronous session. I really enjoyed that time because even if we didn’t have a class, it was still productive. We get to expose ourselves to different kinds of games that will test our skills in identifying the current color for a certain logo; the proper spacing of each character in a word; font sizes, spacing, and padding; and our knowledge of font interfaces.


Game 1: Kern Type, the kerning game
This game allows the players to arrange the characters of each word/font to its proper place in respect to its spacing. I liked this game because it’s simple, and most of it I got it almost perfectly. It was fun to try because I am dead serious playing this game — I almost used my ruler to properly measure and assign the spaces in between of the characters. I lost my focus on the mid-part of game because that’s the hardest parts. Most of that part involves characters that have a thick typography, so it’s really hard to distinguish the spaces. I enjoyed playing the game and I got a score of 94 out of 100 for the whole game.


Game 2: Brandseen – How well do you recognize these brands?
This is my favorite game of all because it involves colors and visual design. I loved this game because it really caught my attention. I was really challenged to perfectly match the right color of the logos of the company we usually knew. At first, I try my best to match the colors. If I’m not really sure about the color of the logo, I google it and I match it with accuracy. It was really hard to find the right color because we were used to type in the hex colors of things. But since the palette only involves dragging your way though to your chosen color, it was really challenging — mostly for the dropbox and apple logo because I think there was a bug where the colors are gradually faded. However, I still managed to match it correctly and earned a score of 95%.


Game 3: Better Web Type
This game frustrated me with my answers and scores. Although I did enjoy playing the game, but what I didn’t enjoy is when I got low scores even after I tried my best to answer it to the best I could. Well… it was still educational because even if I had troubles answering everything, I still learned something new. And this game taught me that no matter how I think I see things correctly; I should still look again and double check everything. I scored 82/100 — it’s not that high but at least I still managed to do it with my best effort.


Game 4: Type War
 This one’s a little bit different than the rest. This game just makes us choose between two fonts to correctly identify the typography or typeface of the letter. Although it still tests our skills and knowledge about the different typefaces, I did enjoy playing the game. In fact, the game doesn’t have an end. It just goes on and on until I suddenly got bored with it. I managed to score a high score in the game and ended with level 7. It was not that easy to reach that level but I’m glad that I stayed playing the game for minutes because I had nothing else better to do. I got a 12 streak of correct answers and I was really educated to recognize and be familiar with the typefaces present in almost everywhere.


Overall, the games helped me, and it was really educational. I’m really glad that people come up with these things to gamify something educational for students and people to engage into. In this way, it removes the boring typical learning setting and pokes interest within the players. I am very thankful to experience these games and I will use what I learned from these in my next projects to come.
